How to Measure Your Bra Size Accurately

  1. Band Size: Measure around your ribcage, just under your bust. Keep the tape snug but not tight.
  2. Bust Size: Measure around the fullest part of your bust, keeping the tape parallel to the ground.
  3. Best Time: Measure at the end of your menstrual cycle when breasts are smallest.
  4. Proper Posture: Stand straight with arms at your sides.

Professional Fitting Tips

  • Sister Sizes: If 34C doesn't fit, try 32D (smaller band, bigger cup) or 36B (bigger band, smaller cup)
  • Signs of Poor Fit: Straps digging in, band riding up, cups gapping or spillage
  • When to Re-measure: Weight changes of 10+ lbs, pregnancy, or every 6 months
  • Shopping Tips: Sizes vary between brands - always try on before buying
  • Professional Fitting: Consider getting fitted by a professional at a lingerie store

How to Use Our Accurate Bra Size Calculator

Step 1: Measure Your Band Size

Using a soft measuring tape, measure around your ribcage directly under your bust. Make sure the tape is level and snug, but not too tight. This measurement is the foundation of your bra size, so it's important to get it right.

Understanding International Bra Sizing

Shopping online or while traveling can be confusing due to different international sizing systems. Our calculator helps by converting your size automatically, but here is a quick guide to the main differences.

US to UK Bra Size Conversion

US and UK bra sizing are similar for smaller cups (A-D), but they diverge significantly after a DD cup. The UK system uses double letters like FF, GG, and HH, whereas the US system often uses DDD, G, H, and so on. Our calculator correctly handles this conversion for you.

US to European (EU) Bra Size Conversion

The European sizing system is fundamentally different. It uses centimeters for the band size (e.g., 75, 80, 85) instead of inches (34, 36, 38). The cup letters are generally similar to the US system, making band conversion the most critical part.

What Are Sister Sizes?

A sister size is an alternative bra size where the cup volume remains the same, even though the band and cup letter change. This concept is incredibly useful if your recommended size doesn't fit perfectly or is out of stock.

How Our Sister Size Calculator Works

The rule is simple: if you go down one size in the band, you must go up one size in the cup to maintain the same volume (e.g., a 34C's sister size is 32D). Our tool automatically provides these alternatives to give you more fitting options.

Sister Size Chart for USA Sizes

A sister size chart allows you to quickly see your alternatives. For example, if you wear a 36D, your smaller sister size is 34DD, and your larger one is 38C. Knowing these options can make finding a comfortable bra much easier.
